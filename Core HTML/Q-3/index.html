Web Design.

Web design is the process of planning, creating, and maintaining websites. It encompasses a variety of disciplines, including graphic design, user experience (UX) design, and interface design (UI). Web designers typically use a variety of tools and software to create websites, including Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and Sketch.
The goal of web design is to create websites that are visually appealing, easy to use, and effective in achieving their goals. Websites can serve a variety of purposes, such as informing visitors, promoting products or services, or generating leads. Web designers must consider a variety of factors when designing a website, including the target audience, the purpose of the website, and the budget.

Web Development.

Web development is the process of creating, building, and maintaining websites and web applications. Web developers typically specialize in front-end development, back-end development, or full-stack development. Front-end developers are responsible for the design and user experience (UX) of a website. They code the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that make up a website's visible elements.
